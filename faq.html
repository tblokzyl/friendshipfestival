Frequently Asked Questions

  • When is the Friendship Festival?
    The Friendship Festival runs from July 12th – July 15th

  • Where is the Friendship Festival?
    The Friendship Festival is held at the Mather Arch Park in Fort Erie. We are an outdoor festival and run rain or shine.

  • What does it cost to attend the Friendship Festival?
    The Friendship Festival is free to attend! Amusement rides tickets, merchandise, food, and alcohol tickets are also available for purchase.

  • Is there a cost for parking?
    There is no cost for parking.
    All vehicles can only park up to the barricades.

  • Which entrance can i use to get to the festival?
    All visitors will need to come through the Queen St. Entrance. The Central Blvd entrance is for accessibility drivers ONLY.

  • Is there a Festival's Got Talent this year?
    No, we have decided not run this event.

  • Why was the date to the Friendship Festival moved away from Canada Day Weekend?
    Many changes have happened over the last few years including less funding from grants. Due to that and the competition of many midway companies, we've found the new dates work best.

  • What rides will be at the festival?
    We only have a tentative list from Robertson’s Amusements Company. We will know for sure as they proceed to set up for the event. At that time, we will post on our Website and Facebook about our official list. We apologize for the delay and thank everyone for their patience.

  • I am interested in being a vendor for next year's festival. What should I do?
    We have a Vendor Application you can fill out right here on our website. If you'd like to know our available pricing options we've outlined them on our Pricing Page.

  • What kind of events are at the Friemdship Festival?
    Thursday evening is our preview night, we mainly have the amusement rides, vendors, food, Shakermaze and a Canada’s Speaker Corner hosted by the Mayor’s Advisory Youth Committee. Friday and Saturday evenings, we have our free concert series and a licensed area. Throughout the weekend, we have a fun zone for the children and teens with a variety of crafts and activities running, as well as our amusement rides, vendors, food, Shakermaze, Canada’s Speaker Corner, and Community Groups attending or holding demonstrations. Lastly, on Sunday, we will be hosting our Canada 150 celebration with a variety of cultural groups and performances.
